Japanese Curry

______________________________________________________________________________________________________

______________________________________________________________________________________________________

History

Curry was introduced to Japan during the Meiji era (1868–1912). At the time the Indian subcontinent was under

British colonial rule. Anglo-Indian officers of the Royal Navy brought the spice mix called curry powder to Japan.

It was classified as yōshoku (Western food) since it came from the West.

______________________________________________________________________________________________________

Description

Japanese curry is commonly served in rice, udon and bread thus making it one of the most popular dish in Japan.

Alongside the source, the dish includes a wide variety of vegetables and meats are used to make Japanese curry such as:

Onions, carrots, and potatoes while beef, pork, for the basic vegetables and chicken are the most popular meat choices.

To improve the taste even more, I also use "secret" ingredients which is optional but I highly recommended it especially for the bold characters.

______________________________________________________________________________________________________

Ingredients

  • 4 Curry Bricks (one package contains 8 bricks)
  • 2 Carrots
  • 2 Potatoes
  • 300 G of Meats of Choice
  • Rice (preferred Japanese Rice)

Secret Ingredients

  • Honey (Sweetness)
  • Apple (Sweetness)
  • Soy Sauce
  • Worcestershire Sauce (Richness)
  • Coffee Powder (Richness)

    • As well as, many other condiments; the point is that it is like seasoning your food.

______________________________________________________________________________________________________

Steps

  1. Slice meats of choices into a cube size and onion into small pieces.
  2. Then, stir fly onions on cooking pot until its colour is yellow-brown.
  3. After that, put meats of choice into cooking pot, stir fly it until its rear is reasonable seared.
  4. Pour water into the cooking pot, enough to boil something.

    5. Tip: Remove strain that come meats on boiling water.

  5. Slice carrots and potatoes while waiting for meat to be cooked.
  6. When meat is reasonably cooked, put all of vegetables into the cooking pot.
  7. Wait for about 20-30 minutes on low heat.
  8. Put curry bricks into the cooking pot.
  9. Stir it until all of the curry bricks melt.

    10. Tip: If your curry is still watery, you can use more brick but be wary since it can taste too strong.

  10. Add your desired "secret" ingredients.
  11. Serve it on hot rice and enjoy!
