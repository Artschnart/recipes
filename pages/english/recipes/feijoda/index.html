______________________________________________________________________________________________________

History

It is widely believed that the beginnings of feijoada stem from slavery in Brazil, where slaves put together the scraps of leftover beans and meat from their owners to create a kind of stew.

However, academics have recently challenged this theory, claiming the origin of feijoada comes from the European settlers.

______________________________________________________________________________________________________

Description

This is a strew dish that contain black beans and variety of meats of choices. The dish is cooked in large quantity and difficult to freeze it overnight.

So, I recommended to cook it for group of people and not for one meal for one person.

______________________________________________________________________________________________________

Ingredients

  • 1 KG of Black Beans
  • 300 G of Beef or Pork
  • 0.5 Purple Cabbage
  • 1 Onion
  • Cassava Flour
  • 2-3 Bay Leaves
  • Papika
  • Rosemary
  • Garlic Powder
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Spicy Mayonnaise
  • Butter or Oil (optional)

______________________________________________________________________________________________________

Steps

  1. Soak Black Beans overnight.
  2. A day later, start boiling bean on cooking pot for 2 hours on medium-low heat. Always close your pot
  3. While preparing for the bean to be properly cooked, prepare meats by slicing it into medium-seized pieces.
  4. Around 10 minutes before the bean is cooked, stir-fly meat on pan until its colour is brown

    5. Tips: Although oil or butter isn't necessary in this step; you can use it to improve meat's taste.

  5. After the bean is cooked, use your potato marsher to marsh bean inside the cooking pot.
  6. Put cooked meat into the cooking pot.
  7. Seasons strew with salt, pepper, papika, rosemary, and galic powder.
  8. Stir it slowly...
  9. Put on bay leaves on the top, set cooking pot's heat to low and wait for 1 hours.
  10. While waiting, prepare farofa by stir cassava flour rapidly on high heat until it started to look like powder.

    11. Note: Farofa is toasted cassava that is used in Brazilian meal.

    Tip: Cassava may smells like burning flour at first, do not worry, let it cool down which is also very important.

  11. Around 10 minutes for the strew to be finally cooked, stir-fly purple cabbage on pan with salt. Stir it until your liking.
  12. After strew is cooked, serve it on hot rice alongside stir-fly cabbage; put farofa on strew; and enjoy your meal with spicy mayonnaise sauce!
